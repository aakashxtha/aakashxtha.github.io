About Me

I'm a Biomedical Engineering researcher at Florida International University with a passion for unraveling the complexities of molecular systems through computational approaches. My research journey has taken me to prestigious laboratories including Arizona State University's Biodesign Institute and the National Renewable Energy Laboratory, where I've developed innovative computational methods that bridge theoretical models with practical applications.

My expertise lies at the intersection of molecular dynamics, protein design algorithms, and machine learning. I specialize in creating computational frameworks that accurately model protein behavior and predict functional properties, ultimately accelerating therapeutic development and material innovation.

Currently, I'm pioneering new approaches that integrate protein dynamics into computational enzyme design – a crucial advancement that could transform how we develop new catalysts, therapeutics, and biomaterials. My work aims to reduce the time and resources required for drug discovery while increasing the success rate of candidate molecules.

Aakash Shrestha, Jeremy Mills

Summer RosettaCon 2024, Cle Elum, WA, August 8, 2024

Presented groundbreaking research that addresses a fundamental limitation in current protein design methodologies by integrating dynamic considerations into computational enzyme design frameworks. Developed and implemented a novel computational approach combining PyRosetta with MARTINI coarse-grained simulations, enabling rapid dynamic analysis of enzyme variants.
