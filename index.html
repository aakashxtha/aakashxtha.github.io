About Me

My Journey

As a biomedical engineering student at Florida International University, I've cultivated expertise in computational biology, molecular modeling, and protein engineering. My passion lies at the intersection of computational methods and biological applications, particularly in developing tools and approaches that can accelerate drug discovery and therapeutic development.

Through research experiences at prestigious institutions like Arizona State University's Biodesign Institute and the National Renewable Energy Laboratory, I've developed and implemented novel computational methods to address complex challenges in protein dynamics, enzyme design, and polymer behavior. I'm driven by the potential of making healthcare more accessible through computational innovation.

Research Interests

  • Integration of protein dynamics into molecular modeling
  • Machine learning applications in protein engineering
  • Sustainable materials development through simulation
  • Accelerating drug discovery with computational methods
